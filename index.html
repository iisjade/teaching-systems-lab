Tool suite

Two practical tools for classroom planning

Teaching Systems Lab is built around a simple workflow: make the task more accessible, then clarify how student understanding will be assessed. This prototype explores teacher-facing planning tools for multilingual math classrooms.

Tool 1

Math Language Scaffold Generator

Turns a math task into editable vocabulary supports, sentence frames, Spanish supports, teacher launch language, misconceptions, and checks for understanding.

What the scaffold changes

The goal is not to simplify the math. The goal is to remove avoidable language barriers.

Original task

A jacket originally costs $80. The store marks it down by 25%. What is the sale price? Explain how you know.

Scaffolded launch

First, find the starting price. Then find the amount of the discount. Finally, subtract the discount from the original price to find the sale price.

Students still solve the percent decrease problem and explain their reasoning.

Why this tool exists

Problem

Many math tasks ask students to reason, model, explain, and justify. For multilingual learners, the mathematical demand can become hidden behind dense wording, unfamiliar vocabulary, or unclear expectations for written explanation. Teachers also need clear assessment language that names what understanding looks like.

Design principles

  • Preserve rigor: keep the original reasoning demand intact.
  • Make structure visible: name quantities, representations, and explanation moves.
  • Support teacher judgment: generate editable scaffolds, not fixed answers.
  • Center access: include multilingual supports without treating translation as the whole solution.

What the MVP demonstrates

  • Instructional design for real classroom constraints
  • Teacher-facing product thinking
  • Standards-aligned math language support
  • Editable output that can move into slides, worksheets, rubrics, or lesson plans
  • A two-tool prototype suite with language scaffolding and standards-based assessment support
